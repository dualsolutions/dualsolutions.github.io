about us

dual solutions is the brainchild born from the Stanford dorm room Logan Bell and Nikhil Devanathan shared. We followed similar paths, largely together, from math into optimization, numerical methods, and statistics. Both of us greatly value research into algorithms, but we also share a passion for open source technology and accessible implementations of mathematical tools. As we collaborated on personal projects outside our coursework, we started to brainstorm what a container for the work we did should look like. dual solutions is this and more. It's a place for us to share the projects we work on and monetize our skills without compromising our beliefs on open source and academic knowledge.

The idea behind dual solutions came from the notion of duality in optimization. In essense, the dual of an optimization problem is an alternative problem with the same solution as the first. Even more simply, it's a different way of examining the same problem. In some ways, the idea of suality captures our working relationship as we may work towards the same goal or result, but from different angles.

With dual solutions, we have one directive: to improve and promote the use of numerical tools for optimization and statistics. In practice this takes on two flavors: the tools we share and the services we offer. On this site, we freely and openly host the technical blog posts we write, software tools we develop, and pertinent papers we write together. We do additionally offer our consulting services to assist clients with using tools we've developed or solving problems within our core capabilities. Contact us for more details on rates and services.
